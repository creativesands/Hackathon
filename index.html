What is a Hackathon you ask?

Well here's the definition right off the internet: A hackathon is a gathering of programmers to collaboratively code in an extreme manner over a short period of time. Hackathons are usually at least a few days (or over a weekend) and generally are no longer than a week. While programmers are working under a particular project, the idea is for each developer to be able to work on whatever he or she wants.

This refers to real world experienced developers frying their brains to get some code to work and eventually build something that they otherwise wouldn't have built while amidst their everyday thoughts and chores.

Creativesands Web Apps Hackathon 2013 is a little different, in that it is more of a preview/preparation of a real hackathon because most of us aren't yet real, experienced developers or even hobbyists.

In this Hackathon I wish to introduce you to the art and science of developing and deploying web apps along with tools and best practices that are currently followed by the community. With that done, sometime into the future we should be able to get together and materialize those cool ideas that would otherwise have remained in our heads because we didn't know how to implement them.


By the end of this Hackathon

  • You would've gained usable knowledge about:

    • HTML, CSS and Javascript (Not VTU stuff, this is the real deal)
    • Google Chrome Developer Tools (A Hackers Swiss Army Knife)
    • Git Version Control System (You'll be happy you were introduced to this)
    • Developing and Deploying Web Apps (and even what they really are)

  • You will build:

    A simple and lite web app. You'll start building from day 1, hour 1.

  • You will not get:

    Fancy Participation Certificates: You wouldn't need one if it's just true knowledge that you are seeking; your work proves your mettle right.

  • You will not become:

    An experienced web developer ready to rebuild Facebook all by yourself.
